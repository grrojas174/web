Behavioral adaptations in mice modelling 16p11.2 hemideletion

The 7qF3 region of mice shows high genetic similarity with the 16p11.2 region of humans. Hemideletion of the 7qF3 region is a model for hemideletion of the 16p11.2 region in humans, a genetic deletion associated with neurodevelopmental disorders like autism spectrum disorder. My interest comes from understanding how learning changes as a consequence of this deletion and possible motoric consequences exhibited in neutral and rewarding scenarios.
